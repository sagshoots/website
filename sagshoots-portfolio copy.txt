About SagShoots
<div class="about-text">
                <h1>About SagShoots</h1>
                <p>I am a photographer based in Brooklyn, New York.</p>
                <p>I make images of people and places in the middle of something — transition, reinvention, recovery, return. My work is rooted in presence and truth: seeing people and the world for what they are, not as they're expected to be, so we can imagine what they might become.</p>
                <p>I came to photography to rediscover what it meant to feel alive. What began as a personal practice of noticing the world — through streets, landscapes, and intimate portraiture — became a way to help others see themselves more clearly, especially when life blurs the edges of who they are.</p>
                <p>Alongside my creative work, I've spent my career in content moderation and policy, where I've seen firsthand how images and narratives shape our understanding of reality. That experience deeply informs my photographic practice: I'm attentive to what images say, what they leave out, and how they can restore nuance, dignity, and agency.</p>
                <p>My approach is quiet, intentional, and collaborative. I'm most interested in arrival, presence, and honesty. Together, we create images that feel grounded, expansive, and real — not just documenting a moment, but reflecting something essential back to you.</p>
                <p>My work is about memory, discovery, and embodiment. About making images you can return to when you need to remember who you are — and see the horizon of who you're becoming.</p>

You Are the Horizon
A Restorative Portrait Experience
You Are the Horizon is a guided portrait experience designed for people navigating change: after a difficult chapter, during a moment of rebuilding, or at the edge of something new and expansive.
This is not a traditional photo session. It's an intentional process that uses photography as a mirror: a way to reconnect with your sense of self, recognize your strength, and recognize yourself clearly as you are and as you're becoming.
We begin with a conversation. I'll ask about where you've been, what you're moving through, and what "power" looks like to you right now. That might mean returning to a meaningful place, choosing a location that makes you feel grounded or expansive, or creating a setting that reflects the version of yourself you want to anchor into.
During the session, I guide gently: through posture, presence, and pacing so the images feel embodied rather than posed. The goal isn't to manufacture confidence, but to capture what's already within you.
The final photographs become more than keepsakes. They serve as reference points, something you can return to on days when you feel uncertain and something to celebrate on days when you feel fully yourself. Proof of who you are and who you've grown into.
This experience is for you if:
		you're coming out of a hard season
		you're reclaiming ease, joy, or clarity
		you're entering a new chapter and want to mark it
		you want photographs that feel meaningful, not performative
		you want to see yourself clearly
You are not just looking at an image of yourself. You are looking toward yourself. You are the horizon.
